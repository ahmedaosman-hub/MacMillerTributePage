Malcolm James McCormick

January 19th, 1992 - September 7th, 2018

"No matter where life takes me, find me with a smile."

Biography

Malcolm James McCormick, also known as Mac Miller, was an American rapper and record producer. Miller began his career in Pittsburgh in the year 2007, at the young age of 15. In 2010, he signed a record deal with an independent label, Rostrum Records, and released his mixtapes K.I.D.S (2010) and Best Day Ever (2011). Miller officially released his debut studio album, Blue Slide Park at the end of 2011, which became the first independently distributed debut album to top the US Billboard 200 since 1995.


In 2013, Miller released his second studio album Watching Movies with the Sound Off (2013). He then moved on to signing with Warner Bros. Records in 2014, where he released four studio albums: GO:OD AM (2015), The Divine Feminine (2016), Swimming(2018), and the posthumous Circles (2020). Miller was nominated posthumously for Grammy Award for Best Rap Album. He also produced for various artists, including himself, under the pseduonym Larry Fisherman.


Unfortunately Miller struggled with addiction and substance abuse, which he often referenced in his lyrics. On September 7th, 2018, Miller died from an accidental drug overdose of cocaine, fentanyl, and alcohol at his home, at the young age of 26.
